My bio

Matteo Francia is a fixed-term researcher on the tenure track (RTT) at the University of Bologna, where he earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering. His teaching subjects are big data, cloud platforms, and machine learning. His research focuses on automated analytics and smart data platforms, with applications to mobility data and precision agriculture. He has authored more than 50 papers in international conferences and journals, including Information Systems, Information Systems Frontiers, IEEE TKDE, DOLAP, ADBIS, ER, and EDBT, for which he received the best demo paper award in 2021. He served as program chair at DATAPLAT (2022-2024), as tutorial chair at ADBIS 2026, and as guest editor for the "Future Generation Computer Systems", "Information System Frontiers", and "Journal of Big Data" international journals (ranked Q1). He was invited professor (3 months) at the University of Tours (France, 2025), a visiting professor at the University of Tours (France, 2024) funded by the Erasmus+ Teaching programme, and a visiting scholar (4 months) at the University of Queensland (Australia, 2019). He received the MSc and BSc with honors from the University of Bologna in 2017 and 2014, respectively. He was awarded by Rotary in 2018 and 2017 as the best graduate in the School of Engineering and Architecture and in Computer Science and Engineering, respectively. He has taken part to national and international projects as a Principal Investigator (UniBO Global South) and participant (WeLASER, PNRR Agritech Spoke 3, Agro.Big.Data.Science), and he collaborated with several companies in the fields of mobility data and precision agriculture.
